### 2. Differentiate from AI content [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/C-bTnMBimlj/) · Aug 2024

`content-strategy`, `google-seo`

**What it does:** Add first-person experience, original data, and specifics to your content so Google doesn't suppress it as AI-generated filler.

**How to execute:**
1. Include original screenshots, data, or case studies in every piece of content
2. Write from first-person experience with specific details AI cannot fabricate
3. Add author bios with verifiable credentials
4. Avoid the generic, authoritative-sounding but substance-free style that AI tools default to
5. Include specific numbers, dates, and named examples rather than generalities

**Why it works:** Google started promoting Reddit because the web got flooded with AI-generated text that sounds authoritative but is factually wrong, so content that proves human origin and real experience gets preferential treatment.

`guerrilla-marketing`, `local-seo`, `copywriting`

**What it does:** Create a simple, ugly-on-purpose flyer with emotional hook, price comparison, and QR code, then distribute it 300+ times near high-density target audience locations.

**How to execute:**
1. Identify your target audience's physical location (e.g. tech workers near Amazon offices)
2. Write a flyer starting with an emotional pain point ('Angry with absurd Uber Eats fees?')

6. Iterate the flyer 5-6 times before mass distribution
7. Spend 3-4 days hanging it up 300+ times in your chosen high-traffic zone

**Why it works:** Placing a compelling offer where your ideal customers physically congregate triggers organic sharing on Reddit, social media, and press coverage without any ad spend.
