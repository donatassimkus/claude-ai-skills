# AI Search / Overviews Hacks

### 96. Exploit AI Overview corrections [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/CtHm1drLaYe/) · Jun 2023

`google-seo`, `keyword-research`, `ai-overviews`

**What it does:** Find queries where Google's AI Overview gives inaccurate or problematic answers and publish authoritative content to become the replacement source.

**How to execute:**
1. Search high-volume emotional, health, or advice queries and screenshot the AI Overview
2. Flag answers that are factually wrong, tone-deaf, or that Google would want to improve
3. Write a comprehensive, cited, expert-level answer to that exact query
4. Use schema markup and E-E-A-T signals (author bio, citations, credentials) to boost authority
5. Recheck periodically to see if Google swaps in your content as the new source

**Why it works:** Google is actively auditing and correcting AI Overview quality issues, so publishing better answers right now gives you a first-mover advantage before the corrections stabilize.

### 159. Build pages for AI queries [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DS0qa__jAb4/) · Dec 2025

`google-seo`, `content-strategy`, `on-page-seo`, `ai-overviews`

**What it does:** Create dedicated pages targeting AI-assisted long-tail searches identified in GSC, structured with H2 sections that directly answer each question.

**How to execute:**
1. Pull the list of 7+ word natural language queries from GSC (see the GSC AI-query mining hacks in this library)
2. Group similar questions by topic or intent
3. Create a unique page per topic cluster
4. Write at least two H2 sections per page that directly answer the questions verbatim
5. Keep answers concise and factual — AI Overviews pull direct answers, not padding
6. Publish and monitor impressions for AI Overview appearances in GSC

**Why it works:** Google's AI mode pulls structured, direct answers from pages — pages built explicitly around natural language questions have a higher chance of being cited in AI Overviews.

### 161. Mine AI prompts via GSC [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DS5eetCjO3F/) · Dec 2025

`google-seo`, `keyword-research`, `technical-seo`, `ai-overviews`

**What it does:** Use Google Search Console to find the exact prompts people type into Google to trigger AI overviews that surface your site.

**How to execute:**
1. Go to Google Search Console
2. Click 'Performance' then 'Search results'
3. Click 'Add Filter' then 'Query'
4. Type 'IAM' (or filter for queries containing AI overview triggers)
5. Review the queries listed — these are actual prompts users typed to get an AI overview featuring your site
6. Copy the language and phrasing patterns from these prompts
7. Incorporate that exact language into your page copy, headers, and FAQs

**Why it works:** Pages that match the conversational language of AI prompts are more likely to be cited in AI overviews, compounding organic visibility.

### 173. Mine AI search queries GSC [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DTV_ss_DJxg/) · Jan 2026

`google-seo`, `keyword-research`, `analytics`, `ai-overviews`

**What it does:** Use Google Search Console to find real AI-assisted queries containing 'I am' to reveal how users describe themselves to Google's AI.

**How to execute:**
1. Go to Google Search Console and connect your site using the Domain property (not URL prefix)
2. Navigate to Performance > Search Results
3. Click 'Query' filter, select 'Queries containing'
4. Type 'I am' and hit Apply
5. Review the full-sentence, conversational queries users are submitting
6. Note the language patterns, self-descriptions, and intent signals
7. Use those phrases and patterns in your content to match AI Overview triggers

**Why it works:** Google's AI search lets users describe their context in natural language, and GSC exposes those exact phrases — giving you a live feed of how your audience talks about themselves and what they need.

### 194. Google Search Console AI query filter [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DVPI5kRjIub/) · Feb 2026

`seo`, `ai-overviews`

**What it does:** Use a Custom RegEx filter in Google Search Console to isolate long-tail AI-style queries (9+ words) driving traffic to your site.

**How to execute:**
1. Open Google Search Console
2. Go to Search Results report
3. Click 'Add Filter'
4. Select 'Custom (Regex)'
5. Enter a regex that matches queries over 9 words (e.g. `^(\S+\s){9,}\S+$`)
6. Scroll to 'Top Queries' to see the filtered results
7. Copy the language and phrasing used in those long queries
8. Incorporate that phrasing naturally into your page content and headings

**Why it works:** AI Overviews pull content that directly matches the conversational phrasing users type into AI-assisted searches, so mirroring that language increases your chances of being cited.

### 195. GSC AI bottom-funnel query filter [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DVjyvEVDBwA/) · Mar 2026

`seo`

**What it does:** Use Google Search Console's new AI-powered performance report customization to surface high-intent queries your site already ranks for.

**How to execute:**
1. Open Google Search Console and go to Search Results
2. Click the new 'Customize your performance report using AI' button
3. Enter prompt: 'Show me bottom of funnel queries'
4. Click Apply to filter results
5. Toggle on Average Position column
6. Filter: Position is greater than 3
7. Export the resulting list of high-intent queries (buy, price, deal, discount, coupon, review, best, vs, comparison, download, sign up, get a quote, contact, near me, shipping cost, order, purchase, subscribe, trial, demo)

**Why it works:** Google already associates your site with these high-intent terms, so minor on-page reinforcement moves you from position 4-20 to top 3 with minimal effort.
