# Founder Brand KB (Distilled)

Reference for the personal-brand skill. Covers professional-network profile optimization, posting cadence, founder-led distribution, thought leadership content engines, and audience building. Load this when the task involves profile optimization, daily or weekly posting systems, founder-led distribution, or a content engine.

Scope note on platforms: the profile, cadence, and ads tactics below are written against LinkedIn, because they depend on that platform's actual features (the Featured section, thought leader ads, its consistency-weighted feed). A reader on a different professional network should substitute the nearest equivalent surface and treat the platform-specific numbers as directional. The frameworks, playbooks, and anti-patterns transfer to any platform.

All examples are illustrative, never hardcoded defaults. Where a case study is called for, use one from the person's own work or a public example they can verify, never an unverified secondhand number.

## Frameworks

### 1. Profile as Landing Page

Stop treating the profile like a CV. Treat it as a conversion asset with banner, headline, About section, and Featured section each doing a specific job.

- **Banner**: visual answer to "what do I do and for whom". Plain-text photos lose.
- **Headline**: role + company + outcome phrased for the buyer, not the HR filter. Template: `[Name], [role] at [company]: [capability] for [outcome]`. Example pattern: `founder at [company]: [service] for revenue not vanity`.
- **About section**: address the top 2-3 pain points the ICP actually names on sales calls. Back with testimonials. End with a CTA.
- **Featured section**: pin the newsletter, podcast, case study, or pricing page. Use the platform's website-link feature if the account tier includes it.
- **Social proof near the top**: podcast guest appearances, press logos, customer quotes.

Why it works: most profile visitors are warm buyers or partners who already saw the content. They are checking whether the person is legitimate. A CV profile makes them pause; a landing page profile makes them click.

### 2. 5x Weekly Posting Cadence (plus 1 Long-Form per Week)

The minimum viable founder content system:
- 5 posts per week on the primary professional network.
- 1 long-form piece per week (video, podcast episode, or blog post).

Daily posting beats 5x posting when it is compatible with the workload. Operators who post seven days a week consistently report that weekend posts pull well because competition is lower, so the marginal post is cheap reach.

The feed rewards consistency over volume. One good post per day compounds faster than irregular high-volume bursts.

### 3. Founder-Led Distribution Flywheel

Pipeline built without paid ads, sequenced in three layers:

1. **Warm the audience.** The founder posts daily. Long-form video or podcast weekly. ICPs discover, follow, binge.
2. **Promote the winners.** Identify the top-performing organic posts. Run them as thought leader ads to two audiences: a website retargeting list, and a cold ICP list.
3. **Layer outbound on top.** Multi-profile connection requests (roughly 100 per week per profile, across founders, sales, support, engineering). Cold email at quarterly cadence, each touch with a fresh angle. Cold calling where team size allows.

Result: inbound shows up pre-sold, and outbound converts at a materially higher rate because the prospect recognises the sender. Brand foundations lift outbound conversion.

### 4. Audience-to-Offer Stack

Three layers stacked so audience compounds into revenue without a separate funnel build:

1. **Audience layer**: the professional network for discovery, video or podcast as the binge-watch backend, newsletter as owned email that hedges algorithm risk.
2. **Lead magnet layer**: playbooks, templates, mini-guides, and checklists a stranger would pay for. A CTA in every long-form asset.
3. **Offer layer**: book-a-call, product signup, or paid community. The profile links directly to it, and the Featured section surfaces it without scrolling.

The newsletter is the hedge: when platform reach drops, and it will, the owned email list keeps the flywheel running. Grow it by adding a CTA to every video, episode, and post.

### 5. Same Message, Many Angles

To become known for one thing, repeat 4-5 core messages from a very large number of angles across every channel. The best content creators say the same things over and over without the audience noticing.

How: define 4-5 core positions. Brainstorm 50+ angles, story hooks, frames, and counterpoints per message. Rotate indefinitely. Only the deeply embedded follower notices the repetition.

Outcome: become "the X person" in the category, where X is a specific method, constraint, or belief. That mental shortcut is what drives inbound.

## Playbooks

### Playbook 1: Profile Optimization Cycle

Run quarterly, or after a positioning shift.

- **Step 1 (30 min).** List the top 2-3 pain points from the last 10 sales calls, or from ICP research if there are no calls yet.
- **Step 2 (1 hour).** Rewrite the About section. Open with the pain, show the outcome delivered, paste 2 customer quotes, end with a CTA.
- **Step 3 (15 min).** Update the headline. Template: `[name] [role] at [company]: [specific capability] for [specific buyer outcome]`.
- **Step 4 (30 min).** Rebuild the Featured section: newsletter signup, best case study, best podcast appearance, product or booking link.
- **Step 5 (15 min).** Refresh the banner with a visual that matches the headline.
- **Step 6 (1 hour).** Pull the profile URL into a backlink tool (Ahrefs, Semrush, or similar), find articles that already link to the profile, and message the author asking them to swap the link for the focus page. A useful SEO side effect of an optimization pass.
- **Success metric**: inbound DMs mentioning the profile, and profile-view-to-follow conversion trending up.

### Playbook 2: Founder Video Channel Launch

For founders adding video as the binge-watch backend to their main written channel.

- **Step 1 (1 week).** Pick one topic cluster aligned with the ICP's purchase research. Avoid topics picked by gut or by copying viral creators; those produce wildly inconsistent results. Use searchable topics people look up every month.
- **Step 2 (ongoing).** Format: practical how-to and case studies with real numbers. Not opinion vlogs. Titles outcome-first ("How I automated X with Y" beats "Y tutorial").
- **Step 3 (ongoing).** One video per week minimum. A companion post on the main channel, and short-form clips cut from every video.
- **Step 4 (ongoing).** Pin a lead magnet or newsletter CTA in the description. Treat every video as a top-of-funnel entry point.
- **Step 5 (months).** Layer SEO on the channel and a companion blog after the founder voice is established. Leading with SEO from a cold start is far harder than layering it on once branded mentions exist.

Retention hint: the first 30 seconds is everything. Ship and watch the retention curve. Iterate on hooks.

### Playbook 3: Daily Posting System

The setup that makes a daily cadence sustainable without burning out.

- **Block one session per week (2-3 hours).** Batch-write 5-7 posts in a single sitting. Schedule them natively or with a scheduler.
- **Maintain 4-5 content pillars.** Rotate deliberately so no two consecutive posts hit the same pillar.
- **Keep a running swipe file.** Sales call objections, FAQ, support tickets, customer wins, failures, tool experiments, contrarian takes. When an idea lands, capture it the same day.
- **Reverse-engineer top performers.** Pick one creator to study in the niche. Pull their 20 highest-performing posts, filtering out engagement-bait hooks. Map the structural bones: hook style, line spacing, body length, CTA. Layer the content onto the proven architecture.
- **Engage daily (30-60 min).** Comment on 5 or more niche-relevant posts per day with value-add takes. Not "great post", not a like. A point of view. Prospects reading the comment section click through to the profile.
- **Audit quarterly.** Export the platform analytics, feed them to an AI assistant, and ask which formats performed best. Double down on winners, reserve 20-30% for experiments.
- **Commit 12 months minimum.** Most operators stop within 3 months. Sustained cadence is the moat precisely because almost no one else sustains it.

### Playbook 4: Thought Leadership Content Engine

How to turn one pillar asset into 5-10 derivative posts, compounding distribution across platforms.

- **Step 1.** Build a rough script for one long-form pillar video on an evergreen topic (playbook, case study, deep breakdown).
- **Step 2.** Record and publish to the video platform as the anchor.
- **Step 3.** Convert the script plus added insights into a blog post. This ranks in search and gets cited by AI assistants.
- **Step 4.** Cut multiple short clips from the video for native video on the professional network.
- **Step 5.** Pull 3-5 written posts out of the pillar: a contrarian angle, a step-by-step, a lesson, a result post, a quote pull.
- **Step 6.** Ship one newsletter edition using the same content. Funnel mix: top-of-funnel how-tos, mid-funnel deep dives, bottom-funnel case studies and client interviews.
- **Output**: one pillar produces 5-10 assets, each with its own organic playback.

Caveat: the pillar must be genuinely strong. Repurposing cannot save mediocre content.

## Tactics

### Headline Formulas

- **Outcome + Buyer**: `founder at [company]: [capability] for [specific buyer outcome, not vanity]`.
- **Contrarian specialty**: `[role] helping [buyer type] do [unpopular but correct thing]`.
- **Position stacked with proof**: `[role] at [company]: [N] [buyers] served, [specific outcome]`.

Rule: keep "passionate about X" and "helping companies achieve Y" out of the headline. Buyers filter for outcome and category, not aspiration.

### About-Section Patterns

Structure that converts profile visitors:

1. **Open with the pain** the ICP names on sales calls. Two sentences.
2. **Offer the outcome** in one sentence. What the work delivers.
3. **3 proof points** (customer quotes, numbers, client names as text).
4. **Who you help** (ICP clarity: industry, stage, constraint).
5. **CTA** with a specific next step: newsletter, podcast, book a call.

Keep it under 2,000 characters. Skip the career timeline; the platform renders it below automatically.

### Featured Section Optimization

Pin 4 items in this priority:

1. **Newsletter or lead magnet** (owned-audience capture).
2. **Highest-social-proof case study** (outcome plus named client).
3. **Podcast appearance** (third-party credibility).
4. **Book-a-call or product page** (conversion).

Order matters: Featured loads above the scroll break. Put the thing you most want clicked first.

### Content Pillars (Founder Defaults)

Most founder content works rotated across 4-5 of these:

1. **Build in public**: revenue numbers, team hires, channel performance, what broke.
2. **Case studies with numbers**: "we did X and got Y in Z timeframe".
3. **Contrarian takes**: the unpopular correct opinion in the category.
4. **Tactical playbooks**: step-by-step from your own work.
5. **Behind-the-scenes decisions**: why you built X, chose Y, ended Z.

Every pillar should tie back to the 4-5 core messages (see Same Message, Many Angles).

Avoid: pure motivation, generic advice, and vague thought leadership with no specific number or decision in it.

### Commenting Strategy

Daily 30-60 min on 5 or more posts from:
- Dream clients you want to reach.
- Peers and similar companies in the category.
- Trusted voices the ICP follows.

Every comment must add a point of view: "love this take, my two cents would be X", or "we're seeing this work today". Not "great content", not a like. The prospects reading the comment section click through to the profile.

### DM Flow (Inbound)

When someone comments or reacts on a post:
1. **Send a thank-you** on the first meaningful comment. Light, human.
2. **Follow up 2-3 days later** with something useful, not a pitch: "saw you commented on my X post, here's a related playbook I wrote".
3. **Ask a qualifying question** if the profile fits ICP: "what's your current approach to Y?"
4. **Book a call only when they signal intent.** Never open with a calendar link.

Rule: the first DM is give-only. Pitching into comment warmth burns the relationship.

### Brand Recall Engineering

Pair the person's name with a single memorable visual or keyword so prospects recognise them across channels. It can be a recurring emoji, a self-given title, a colour, or one word that appears in every asset. Bake it into the email signature, the profile, and subject lines.

Why: cold outreach open and reply rates rise sharply when the recipient already recognises the sender from their content.

### Connection Growth

Weekly: send roughly 100-200 connection requests to ICP matches, filtered with the platform's own advanced search or sales tier. At team scale, multiple profiles each sending 100 per week compounds quickly.

Do not always message on connect. The connection alone puts future posts in their feed.

### Thought Leader Ads

Take the top-performing organic posts (social proof, case studies, breakdowns) and boost them as thought leader ads to:
- A website visitor retargeting list.
- A cold ICP list, kept tight at roughly 20K-80K audience size. This platform's ad targeting is far less adaptive than the large social ad networks, so wide audiences waste budget.

Also sponsor partner or client posts that mention you. Third-party validation lands harder than self-promotion.

## Anti-Patterns

### Sporadic posting with burst-and-burnout

Posting 10 times one week and 0 the next. The feed treats consistent posters as reliable. Irregular cadence loses the compounding benefit.

Fix: one post per day minimum, or 5x per week on a fixed schedule. Sustainable beats heroic.

### Corporate robot voice

Posts written like a press release. "Excited to announce", "we are thrilled", "changing the way [buyers] do [thing]". Zero specifics, zero opinion, zero human.

Why it fails: nobody trusts an indistinct voice. Buyers choose operators they feel they know. AI-saturated feeds make this worse, because generic content now reads as machine-written.

Fix: first person, specific numbers, real decisions, real mistakes. Build-in-public beats corporate announcement.

### Engagement pods and bait posts

"Comment X for the template", mutual-like chains, and pods that inflate numbers. Platforms detect and down-weight these over time. More important: the audience built from bait is the wrong audience. They came for free stuff, not for the offer.

Fix: when reverse-engineering top performers, filter to posts that performed without an engagement-bait hook. Optimise for the right eyeballs, not the biggest numbers.

### Vanity follower counts

Chasing follower count with no conversion mechanism. Platforms rank subscribers third or fourth as a source of views, behind recommendations and discovery, so a large following does not guarantee reach. Creators with very large audiences routinely post content that reaches a small fraction of them.

Fix: track inbound DMs, demo bookings, newsletter signups, and partnership requests. Follower count is a lagging signal at best and a vanity trap at worst.

### Generic posting with no founder credibility

A non-founder team member posting the content the founder would post. The words do not carry without the position (founder, bootstrapped, hired, fired, scaled). The same words from a non-founder land flat.

Fix: the founder posts, or the staff voice earns credibility through its own specific position ("I spoke to 40 sales leaders", "I ran the inside sales team for 5 years"). A staff voice works when it is matched to a beat that person genuinely owns.

### Gut-feel video topic selection

Choosing video topics by copying viral creators, or by posting what you personally want to talk about. Results are wildly inconsistent with zero predictability.

Fix: pick topics people actively search for every month, trending or not. Keyword research before you hit publish.

### Forced creator ad reads

Paying a creator to plug a product in a 30-60 second ad break. Viewers tune out. No story context, no recurring exposure.

Fix: embed the product inside the creator's narrative so it appears naturally across many episodes. Story-embedded partnerships materially outperform standard ad reads, and a gifting or philanthropy budget you would spend anyway can fund them.

### Paying employees per post

Incentivises volume over long-term brand building. People post to hit the bonus, not to build a real audience.

Fix: frame it as collaboration. The company funds time and tooling; the employee builds a personal brand and owns the audience. Avoid tying posts to short-term compensation.

### Oversized ad audiences

Applying large-social-network audience thinking to a professional network. Its ad algorithm is far more manual and basic, so wide audiences waste budget.

Fix: keep the audience between roughly 20K-80K. Tighter ICP. Consider uploading a list. Match the platform's actual targeting capability.

## Implementation Sequencing

For an operator starting from zero, in this order:

1. **Week 1.** Optimise the profile as a landing page. Run the quarterly cycle once as a baseline.
2. **Weeks 1-4.** Commit to daily or 5x weekly posting. Write 5 posts in a batch and schedule them. Engage 30 min per day.
3. **Month 2.** Add weekly long-form (video or podcast). Run the content engine on one pillar per week.
4. **Month 3.** Set up a lead magnet and newsletter. Add a CTA to every post and video.
5. **Months 3-6.** Reverse-engineer a top performer's architecture. Layer their structural bones onto your content.
6. **Month 6.** Start thought leader ads boosting top organic posts to retargeting and cold ICP lists.
7. **Month 6+.** Layer SEO on the blog and video channel. Far easier now that branded mentions and entity signals exist.
8. **Month 12+.** Audit quarterly, double down on winners, and maintain cadence as the moat.

Caveat across all of it: most operators stop inside 3 months. The compounding only starts after 6-12 months of consistent output. Sustained cadence is the defensible position.
