# Content Strategy Reference

### 1. Every Page Gets Unique Meta

No two pages should share the same title or description. Search engines need distinct signals for each URL.

**Title tag formula by page type:**
Note: these formulas are structured for local service businesses. For SaaS, ecommerce, or B2B, adapt the pattern: remove location from the formula and lead with the use case, benefit, or audience instead.
- **Home**: `{Primary Service} Across the {Region}`
- **Service page**: `Professional {Service Name} in {Region}`
- **Location page**: `{Primary Service} in {Town}, {Region}`
- **Region page**: `{Primary Service} in {Region}`
- **Hub page**: `{Hub Category} Services in {Region}`
- **Sector page**: `{Service} for {Sector} in {Region}`
- **Blog post**: `{Post Title}`

**Title rules:**
- Max 60 characters (Google truncates beyond this)
- Primary keyword at the start
- Include location qualifier for local businesses
- No keyword stuffing
- Do not append brand name unless there is room and it adds CTR value

**Title CTR optimisation:**
Ranking is only half the battle. The title must earn the click.

- **Sentiment words**: Include a positive or negative sentiment word. Positive: "best", "proven", "trusted", "professional", "reliable". Negative: "avoid", "worst", "dangerous", "warning", "never".
- **Power words**: Words that create urgency, curiosity, or authority. Examples: "essential", "ultimate", "guaranteed", "free", "instant", "expert", "complete".
- **Numbers**: Titles with numbers consistently outperform those without. Use odd numbers for list posts, specific numbers for credibility. Example: `7 Signs You Need a New Bookkeeper`
- Combine tactics where natural: `5 Proven Ways to Fix [Problem] in Your [Context]`
- Do not force these into every title. Service and location page titles should stay clean and keyword-focused. These tactics are most valuable for blog posts and informational content.

**Meta description formula:**
- Max 155 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Include a call to action ("Call today", "Free quote")
- Include a differentiator ("20+ years experience", "certified member")
- Google sometimes rewrites meta descriptions. Write strong, relevant content to reduce the chance of rewrites.

**Common issues to check:**
- Duplicate titles across pages
- Too long (truncated in SERP) or too short (wasted opportunity)
- Keyword stuffing
- Missing entirely
- No compelling reason to click (weak descriptions)


## Content Quality and E-E-A-T

### E-E-A-T Signals

**Experience**
- First-hand experience demonstrated
- Original insights and real examples
- Work with subject matter experts for highly credible content

**Expertise**
- Industry credentials visible
- Accurate, detailed information
- Author bylines and author bios/pages with links to social profiles
- Transparent editorial review process

**Authoritativeness**
- Industry accreditations displayed
- Showcase endorsements, awards, and news mentions
- Build evergreen content that gets naturally referenced and linked by others
- Actively grow online reviews on third-party sites

**Trustworthiness**
- Accurate information
- Transparent about the business
- Contact information easy to find
- Strong About page with mission, team, cited reviews, and testimonials
- Cite and link credible sources in content
- Secure site (HTTPS)

**YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)**

Pages in YMYL categories face stricter quality evaluation from Google. Categories include: Financial Information, Health and Safety, Legal and Government, Shopping and Transactions, Current Events/News. Strong E-E-A-T signals are required for these topics.

### E-E-A-T Checklist
- [ ] Content is factually accurate and positively beneficial for the audience
- [ ] Author bylines with bios and links to social profiles
- [ ] Editorial review process is visible or referenced
- [ ] Credible sources are cited and linked
- [ ] About page includes mission, team, and cited reviews
- [ ] Contact page is easy to find with multiple contact methods
- [ ] Online reviews are growing on third-party sites
- [ ] Endorsements, awards, or news mentions are showcased
- [ ] Evergreen content exists that naturally attracts references
- [ ] Subject matter experts contributed to content where relevant
- [ ] Structured data (JSON-LD) reinforces accurate information signals

### AI Content Strategy

Google does not penalise AI content per se (Google Search Central, February 2023). Google penalises unhelpful, low-quality content regardless of how it was produced.

**Safe zones for AI copy (low risk):**
- Meta descriptions and meta titles
- Disclaimers and utility copy
- Noindex pages
- Content briefs and outlines
- Rough drafts for human editing
- Keyword research assistance

**High risk (avoid):**
- Publishing full AI-generated pages without human editing
- Mass-producing AI articles on autopilot
- Keyword stuffing in AI content
- Fully automated publishing pipelines with no professional oversight

**Best practice:** Use AI to create drafts and assist research, then send through careful manual editing. AI as writing assistant, not writer.

### Entities and Information Gain

**Entities**

An entity is a named person, place, or thing connected to other entities in Google's knowledge graph. Content that incorporates related entities (nouns and ideas closely associated with the main topic) increases relevancy signals. A page about its primary topic should naturally mention closely related entities.

**Information Gain**

Google's Information Gain patent scores how much new information a document provides beyond information contained in documents the user has already seen. Pages with higher information gain scores may be ranked higher.

**Formula:** Entities (relevancy) + Information Gain (uniqueness) = strongest ranking signal.

**Ways to achieve information gain:**
- Incorporate proprietary data and research
- Find angles in search intent that competitors are not covering
- Curate more than competitors (reviews, case studies, examples, resources)
- Add clearer, more actionable steps
- Add calculators, tools, checklists, or templates to the page
- Provide deeper historical context on the topic


### Table of Contents

A Table of Contents (ToC) improves readability and can generate jump-link sitelinks in Google search results.

**When to add a ToC:**
- Blog posts over approximately 1,000 words
- Service pages or guides with 4+ major sections
- Any long-form content where users benefit from quick navigation

**Implementation rules:**
- Auto-generate from H2 and H3 headings on the page
- Place after the introductory paragraph, before the first H2
- Use anchor links that jump to the corresponding heading
- Make the ToC collapsible on mobile
- Use `<ol>` for sequential content (how-to guides) or `<ul>` for non-sequential content (topic overviews)

**SEO benefits:**
- Google may display ToC links as sitelinks beneath your search result, increasing SERP real estate and CTR
- Improves time on page
- Reduces bounce rate on long-form content
- Signals well-structured content to search engines

### Content Depth and Quality

- Comprehensive coverage of topic
- Answers follow-up questions
- Better than top-ranking competitors
- Updated and current
- Sufficient depth for topic (see Content Length Benchmarks section)
- Keyword in first 100 words
- Related keywords used naturally
- Content is not limited to text: multimedia SEO includes video, images, and podcast content

**Formatting rules:**
- **Maximum 120 words per paragraph**: any paragraph exceeding 120 words should be split
- Short paragraphs, bullet lists, and subheadings break up content
- Use tables for comparisons or structured data
- Bold key terms on first use within a section
- Direct writing style: no-fluff, no filler words, no passive voice
- Avoid filler openers: "In this article", "Let's dive in", "We'll explore"

### High-Quality Content Checklist
- [ ] On-topic, well-researched, and factually accurate
- [ ] Optimised for E-E-A-T
- [ ] Human-written, edited, and fact-checked
- [ ] Incorporates related entities corresponding to the main topic
- [ ] Adds information gain beyond existing articles on the same topic
- [ ] Cites credible sources
- [ ] Writing style is direct and digestible (no walls of text)
- [ ] Includes multimedia where appropriate

## Page-Type SEO Patterns

### Service Pages

Target "{service name}" and "{service name} near me" searches.

**Must include:**
- H1: exact service name
- Title: "Professional {Service} in {Region}"
- Detailed description of the service (300+ words)
- Features/benefits list
- Breadcrumbs linking to hub
- Related services for internal linking
- JSON-LD: Service schema, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage if FAQ section exists

### Location Pages

Target "[service] in [city]" and "[service] near me" searches.

**Must include:**
- H1: "{Service} in {Location}"
- Title: "{Service} in {Location}, {Region}"
- Body copy mentioning the location naturally 3-5 times
- List of services available in that location
- Nearby locations linked
- Local knowledge (mention landmarks, areas, local context)
- Embed Google Map
- JSON-LD: LocalBusiness schema with address and geo coordinates

### Hub Pages

Target broad category terms (e.g., "[service category]" or "[primary service type]").

**Must include:**
- H1: "{Category} Services"
- Overview of all services in the category
- Links to every child service page
- Brief description of each child service

### Sector Pages

Target "{service} for {sector}" searches.

**Must include:**
- H1: "{Service} for {Sector}"
- Body copy addressing sector-specific risks and concerns
- Relevant services for that sector
- Sector-specific trust signals (compliance with regulations, industry standards)
- Links to relevant service and location pages
- Case studies or examples from that sector where available

## SEO Testing and Content Refreshes

SEO testing is the systematic application of test-measure-learn to organic search. Every content refresh is an SEO test.

### Common SEO Tests

- **Title tag testing**: Change title, measure CTR change in Search Console over 4-13 weeks.
- **Content refresh**: Rewrite or significantly update existing page content.
- **Internal link changes**: Add or restructure internal links and measure ranking changes.
- **Structured data additions**: Add or modify JSON-LD and measure rich snippet appearance.

### Content Refresh Process (6 Steps)

1. **Identify**: Find pages with performance decay or upside potential (ranking positions 4-20).
2. **Benchmark**: Record current ranking on primary terms, traffic over the last 90 days, and conversions.
3. **Analyse**: Perform SERP analysis on the target keyword. Create a new content brief.
4. **Rewrite**: Write and publish updated content based on the new brief.
5. **Resubmit**: Submit the updated URL in Google Search Console for re-indexation.
6. **Measure**: Wait 4-13 weeks. Compare new performance metrics to the original baseline.

### Testing Types

- **Time-based testing**: Compare before/after performance. Use Bayesian decision intervals (75% or 65% confidence) rather than requiring 95%+ statistical significance.
- **A/B split testing**: Only feasible for programmatic pages at scale where you have enough similar pages to split into test and control groups.
- **Bulk tests**: Apply the same change across many similar pages and measure aggregate impact.

## Programmatic SEO

Programmatic SEO is a scalable approach to building and deploying large arrays of web pages using templates and a database.

### Playbook Patterns

| Playbook | Pattern | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Locations | "{service} in {location}" | "Accountant in Leeds" |
| Personas | "{service} for {audience}" | "SEO for restaurants" |
| Glossary | "what is {term}" | "What is a canonical tag" |
| Comparisons | "{X} vs {Y}" | "WordPress vs Squarespace" |
| Templates | "{tool} template" | "Invoice template for freelancers" |
| Directory | "{category} in {location}" | "Web designers in Manchester" |

### Building a Programmatic Strategy

1. **Find a patterned keyword category** with repeatable search intent.
2. **Ideate content and database strategy**: What unique value can the business provide?
3. **Build database and content brief**: Ensure data can be pulled and deployed to each URL.
4. **Organise hubs and internal links**: Categorise pages, plan bi-directional linking.
5. **Create content**: Some pages need only short paragraphs (from database), others need full pages written by a professional with AI assistance plus human editing.
6. **Wireframe and design**: Design with UX, mobile display, and accessibility in mind.
7. **Develop and deploy**: Test for quality assurance and audit for SEO.
8. **Ensure proper indexation**: Often the biggest challenge for programmatic SEO, especially with lower domain authority.
9. **Measure and report**: Track KPIs over the lifetime of the pages. Not set-and-forget.
10. **Test and re-optimise**: Programmatic pages are the only area where true A/B split testing can be executed at scale.

### Indexation Tips for Large Page Arrays

- Ensure main content loads properly and is not blocked by resources
- Verify meta robots and canonical tags are correct and indexable for all pages
- Check robots.txt is not blocking any pages
- Add programmatic pages to an XML sitemap and submit in Google Search Console
- Build strong internal links throughout the entire programmatic strategy
- Increase domain authority through external link acquisition
- No-value pages should be noindexed rather than left as thin content

### Common Programmatic SEO Mistakes

- **Thin content**: Just swapping city/keyword names in otherwise identical content. Every page must provide unique value.
- **Keyword cannibalization**: See dedicated section below.
- **Over-generation**: Creating pages for keyword combinations with no search demand.
- Quality over quantity: 50 genuinely useful pages beats 500 thin ones.

## Blog Content Creation Pipeline

Follow this pipeline when creating blog posts and long-form content pages.

### Content Creation Workflow

1. **Research**: Conduct structured research on the target keyword.
   - Source quality rules: prefer standards bodies, official documentation, government sources, universities, and reputable media.
   - Use at least 10 unique source domains, with at least 4 non-vendor domains.
   - Avoid affiliate roundups, scraped statistics pages, and content with no author or date.
   - Attach source URLs directly to each fact or bullet.
2. **Plan**: Produce a structured SEO planning pack (see Content Planning Framework below).
3. **Write**: Draft the content following the Blog Writing Rules below.
4. **Internal link injection**: Insert internal links following the Blog Internal Linking Rules.
5. **External link injection**: Insert outbound links following the Blog External Linking Rules.
6. **Image creation**: Create featured and inline images.
7. **Slug and meta description**: Generate the URL slug and meta description.

### Content Planning Framework

Produce a planning pack for every blog post before writing begins.

**Target and purpose:**
- Primary keyword
- Search intent (informational / transactional / navigational)
- Funnel stage (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU)
- User job: what the reader is trying to accomplish
- Secondary keywords with mapping to H2 sections
- Terminology map: primary term, acceptable variants, terms to avoid

**SERP reality check:**
- Dominant page type in current results (listicle, how-to, service page, etc.)
- Common promise in top titles
- SERP features present (PAA, featured snippet, local pack)
- Top competing URLs

**Intent lock:**
- One-sentence intent statement defining what the page IS about
- Not-intent boundaries listing what the page is NOT about

**Uniqueness gate:**
- Check existing site content to ensure no page already targets this keyword
- If overlap exists, decide: merge, differentiate, or skip

**Required topical coverage:**
- Must-cover subtopics
- PAA (People Also Ask) questions to answer
- Snippet target: target question + preferred format (paragraph, list, table)
- Entities and related terms to include

**Information gain plan:**
- What unique value this post adds beyond competitors
- Sources of uniqueness: proprietary data, field experience, case studies, original photos, practical checklists

**E-E-A-T notes:**
- Ideal author profile
- Examples needed (real job scenarios, local area references)
- Expert review suggestion

**Scope boundaries:**
- In-scope topics
- Out-of-scope topics
- Target audience (e.g., homeowners, small business owners, first-time buyers)
- Not-for audience (e.g., enterprise procurement teams, industry specialists)

**Facts and sources:**
- Standard facts with source URLs and evidence snippets
- Claims that need citations
- All facts must come from research. Never invent statistics, limits, or claims.

**YMYL safety check:**
- If the content touches health and safety, financial decisions, legal matters, or other YMYL categories, note any safety disclaimers needed
- Flag content that requires expert review before publication
- Example: "Always consult a qualified professional before acting on advice in this article"

**Page lock (final deliverables):**
- Final URL slug
- SEO title (max 60 chars)
- H1
- Meta description (max 155 chars)
- Opening promise (first sentence hook)
- Target word count

### Blog Writing Rules

**Hard rules:**
- Follow the SEO plan exactly for intent, scope, and structure
- If the plan provides exact H2s and order, use them verbatim
- Target 1,000-2,500 words unless the plan specifies otherwise. Never pad with fluff to hit a word count.
- Do not invent facts, statistics, treatment timescales, pricing, or claims not in the plan
- Promotional references to your own services are natural and encouraged where relevant. Keep the balance: the article should primarily inform, with service mentions fitting naturally into context. Dedicated CTA blocks work best at the end or after key sections.
- Do not recommend or endorse competitor services unless the plan explicitly requires it
- Do not print source URLs in the article body

**Tone and style:**
- Professional but conversational
- Avoid filler openers: "In this article", "Let's dive in", "We'll explore", "This guide will"
- Do not use em dashes
- Use short paragraphs (maximum 120 words per paragraph), bullet lists, and subheadings
- Include real-world examples where possible

**Formatting:**
- H1: article title (exactly one per page)
- H2: major sections
- H3: subsections within H2s (optional, use only when genuinely needed)
- Do not skip heading levels
- Bold key terms on first use within a section
- Use tables for comparisons or structured data
- Include a Table of Contents for posts over 1,000 words (per ToC rules in this skill)

### Slug Creation Rules

- Use the article title as the primary source
- Lowercase only, hyphens between words
- Maximum 60 characters
- No leading or trailing hyphens
- Do not include generic words: "blog", "guide", "article", "post"
- Always remove articles: "a", "an", "the"
- **Intent-first**: if the title starts with "How to", the slug must start with `how-to-`. If the title starts with a question word (What, Why, When, Can, Do, Is, etc.), the slug must start with that word.
- **Phrase integrity**: keep compound terms together. "Web design" stays as `web-design` (never split with inserted words).
- **Uniqueness**: before publishing, check the slug against existing site URLs. If it already exists, append a meaningful modifier (e.g., `prevention`, `signs`, `treatment`, `cost`, `checklist`, `faq`). Do not add numbers.

## International and Multilingual SEO

**International SEO vs Multilingual SEO — not the same thing:**
- **International SEO**: targeting multiple countries. Same language, different countries (e.g. US English vs UK English). Primary goal is geotargeting.
- **Multilingual SEO**: targeting the same country in multiple languages (e.g. French and English in Canada). Primary goal is language targeting.

Both require hreflang tags and careful URL structure planning. They can also overlap — targeting multiple countries in multiple languages.

### URL Structure Options

This is the most critical decision before building. Three options:

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **CCTLDs** (yourbrand.ca) | Fully separate experience, strongest local signal | DA starts at 0, expensive, very hard to scale beyond 2-3 countries |
| **Subdomains** (fr.yourbrand.com) | More localized feel, centralized CMS maintenance | DA harder to consolidate vs subdirectories, more effort to geolocate |
| **Subdirectories** (yourbrand.com/fr/) | Strongest DA consolidation, fastest to deploy, easiest to maintain centrally | Slightly less localized appearance |

**Recommendation:** Subdirectories are best for most teams. CCTLDs are not available for same-country multilingual targeting.

Do not use parameterized URLs (yourbrand.com?lang=fr). Google does not reliably use these for geotargeting.

### hreflang Tags

hreflang tags tell search engines which language and country each page targets.

```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-gb" href="https://example.co.uk/service" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="de" href="https://example.de/dienstleistung" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://example.com/service" />
```

**Rules:**
- Use ISO 639-1 (language codes, lowercase, e.g. `en`, `de`, `fr`) and optionally ISO 3166-1 Alpha 2 (country codes, uppercase, e.g. `en-GB`, `fr-CA`)
- Every page must list hreflang alternates for all versions, including a self-referencing tag
- Use `hreflang="x-default"` for the fallback page
- Every page in language A must link to its equivalent in language B, and vice versa (bidirectional)
- Canonical tags and hreflang tags must be consistent — canonical should be self-referencing
- Use absolute URLs, not relative URLs
- Embed in `<head>`, HTTP headers, or the XML sitemap

Translate all on-page SEO elements: title tags, meta descriptions, alt text, URL slugs, and body content.

### User Experience Warning

Do not automatically redirect users to a country or language version based on IP address or browser language detection. Google explicitly discourages this — it prevents Googlebot from crawling all versions of your content.

Instead: show a language or location switcher in the header or footer and let users choose. Offer a prominent prompt if you detect a likely mismatch (e.g., "Are you visiting from France? Switch to FR"), but never force the redirect.

### Translation Options

| Option | Quality | Speed | Cost | When to use |
|--------|---------|-------|------|-------------|
| AI-only | Low (error-prone) | Fastest | Cheapest | Last resort for very large page counts with no budget |
| Human / agency | Highest | Slowest | Most expensive | Best if you can afford it |
| Hybrid (AI draft + native editor) | Good | Semi-fast | Semi-affordable | Best for most teams |

Never publish AI-only translation for important pages. Native speaker review is non-negotiable for money pages and YMYL content.

## Content Length Benchmarks by Page Type

Content quality always outweighs quantity. These are targets, not rigid rules. Never pad content to hit a number.

| Page Type | Minimum | Target | Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Blog post (informational) | 800 | 1,500-2,500 | Long-form guides and tutorials benefit from 2,000+ words |
| Service page | 300 | 500-800 | Focused on the service, benefits, process, and CTA |
| Location page | 200 | 400-600 | Location-specific content, services available, local context |
| Hub page | 200 | 400-600 | Overview with links to child pages |
| Sector page | 300 | 500-800 | Sector-specific risks, services, compliance |
| FAQ page/section | 100 per Q&A | 5-10 Q&As | Each answer should be substantive (50-150 words) |
| Product page (e-commerce) | 200 | 300-500 | Description, features, specifications |

## Output format

**For an audit:**

Structure findings as follows:

**Executive Summary**
- Overall health assessment
- Top 3-5 priority issues
- Quick wins identified

**Findings** (one entry per issue):
- **Issue**: What is wrong
- **Impact**: High / Medium / Low
- **Evidence**: How you found it
- **Fix**: Specific recommendation

**Prioritised Action Plan**
1. Critical fixes (blocking indexation or ranking)
2. High-impact improvements
3. Quick wins (easy, immediate benefit)
4. Long-term recommendations

**For keyword research:**
- Prioritised list with: keyword, monthly volume, intent, difficulty estimate, recommended page type, CTA

**For a content plan:**
- Topic, target keyword, intent, page type, CTA, internal link targets, estimated traffic upside

**Rules:**
- Propose 10-30 high-impact pages, not 500 filler pages.
- Every suggestion gets a revenue impact estimate using a simple funnel model (state your traffic and conversion assumptions).
- Label assumptions.
- No vanity metrics. Clicks and conversions only.
