# Link Building Reference

### 6. Internal Linking Strategy (Hub-and-Spoke)

Internal links are the most powerful SEO lever you control 100% after content. Search engines interpret site architecture from internal link relationships, not just URL folder structures.

**Link Equity (Link Juice)**

Link equity is the page authority value passed from one page to another via dofollow links. The more links a page has accrued, the more equity it can pass on. Topically related pages pass more value than unrelated ones.

**Bi-directional Linking (Critical)**

Hub pages MUST link to all spoke pages AND spoke pages MUST link back to the hub. This bi-directional linking is the most important pattern. URL folder nesting (e.g. `/hub/spoke` vs `/hub-spoke`) has minimal impact compared to the internal link relationships themselves.

**Link Placement Hierarchy (value order, highest to lowest):**
1. Contextual body links within paragraph text. Links placed higher on a page carry more value.
2. Programmatic modules (e.g. "Related Services" cards, "Nearby Areas" links)
3. Top navigation and footer links
4. Sidebar links
5. Tabs or accordion dropdown links

**Link patterns:**
- Hub pages link to ALL child service pages
- Service pages link back to parent hub
- Service pages link to 3-5 related services (sibling pages)
- Location pages link to top services available in that area
- Location pages link to nearby locations
- Footer links to top services, sectors, and primary locations
- Secondary footer navigation with additional internal links to flatten architecture
- Breadcrumbs on every page below home level
- Blog posts link to relevant service pages contextually

**Anchor text rules:**
- Use descriptive anchor text: "drain cleaning services" not "click here"
- Vary anchor text slightly across pages to avoid over-optimisation
- Include location in anchor text where relevant

**Robinhood Strategy for Link Equity Distribution**
1. Identify "poor pages": underperforming pages or money pages that need more authority.
2. Identify "rich pages": pages with the highest quantities of external backlinks (use Ahrefs, Semrush, or Search Console).
3. Add internal links from rich pages to poor pages to redistribute equity. Only link relevant, topically related content.

**Sitewide Links**

Top nav and footer links appear on every page. The more sitewide links you incorporate, the flatter your architecture becomes for SEO, making more pages reachable within fewer clicks.

**Crawl Depth**

How many clicks from the homepage does a crawler need to reach a page? Fewer clicks = better. Prefer flat architecture (important pages within 2-3 clicks). Orphan pages get no crawl attention and may never be indexed.

**Keyword Clustering (3 Levels)**

Keyword clusters (individual keywords) → Topic clusters (grouped by theme) → URL clusters (hub-and-spoke pages). Research keywords first, group them into topics, then map topics to your URL/page structure.

**Common issues:**
- Over-optimised anchor text
- Important pages buried deep (high crawl depth)
- Broken internal links
- One-directional hub-spoke links (hub links to spokes but spokes don't link back)
- Rich pages not linking to pages that need equity

**Blog internal linking limits per post:**
- Maximum 8 internal links total
- Maximum 2 links to hub/category pages
- Maximum 3 links to service pages
- Maximum 2 links to other blog posts
- Maximum 1 link to location pages
- Do NOT add links in the first 3 paragraphs or the first paragraph under any heading
- Do NOT insert links inside headings

### 7. Outbound Linking Strategy

Outbound links to authoritative, non-competitor sources signal that your content is well-researched and connected to the broader topic.

**General rules:**
- Link to reputable, authoritative sources: government sites, industry bodies, universities, established media
- Never link to competing businesses
- At least one outbound link should be dofollow (signals confidence in the source)
- Use natural, descriptive anchor text (2-5 words)
- Do not insert outbound links inside headings

**By page type:**
- Blog posts: 1-2 outbound links per post
- Service pages: 0-1 outbound links to an industry body, regulation, or authoritative resource
- Location pages: generally no outbound links needed (exception: local council or environmental authority pages)
- Hub pages: 0-1 outbound links to an authoritative category-level resource

**When NOT to link out:**
- No suitable authoritative source exists
- The link would point to a direct competitor
- The source is an affiliate roundup, scraped statistics page, or content with no author or date

### 8. Link Attributes (nofollow, sponsored, UGC)

**`rel="nofollow"`**: Use when you do not want to endorse the linked page or pass link equity.
- Links you are uncertain about the quality or trustworthiness of
- Links in user-submitted content as a fallback if `ugc` is not supported

**`rel="sponsored"`**: Use for any link that exists because of a commercial arrangement.
- Paid partnerships, sponsored content, affiliate links
- Links exchanged as part of advertising or sponsorship deals
- Required by Google guidelines. Failure to mark paid links can result in manual actions.

**`rel="ugc"`**: Use for links within user-generated content.
- Blog comments, forum posts, community submissions
- Any link created by users rather than site owners

**Default (no attribute / dofollow)**: Use for links you genuinely endorse.
- Links to authoritative sources that support your content
- There is no `rel="dofollow"` attribute. The absence of nofollow/sponsored/ugc makes a link followed by default.


## Backlink Building Strategies

Backlinks remain one of the strongest ranking factors. Earning high-quality backlinks builds domain authority and directly improves rankings.

**Foundational tactics:**
- **Create link-worthy content**: Original research, data, tools, comprehensive guides, and unique visual assets naturally attract links. Highest ROI long-term.
- **Outreach and relationship building**: After publishing strong content, reach out to site owners, journalists, and bloggers who cover related topics.
- **Digital PR**: Newsworthy content (surveys, studies, seasonal data, expert commentary) can earn links from media outlets and high-authority publications.
- **Industry directories and citations**: Foundational authority signals.
- **Guest contributions**: Write expert articles for reputable industry publications with a natural link back to relevant content.
- **Reclaim unlinked mentions**: Monitor brand mentions using Google Alerts, Ahrefs, or Mention. If someone references your business without linking, reach out and request a link.

**Grey hat tactics are on the table. Label risk level (low/medium/high).**
- Parasite SEO: publish on high-authority platforms when the math works
- Expired domain redirects: consider when relevant topical authority exists
- Always evaluate: is the time cost worth the link quality?

**Rules:**
- Quality over quantity: one link from a high-authority, topically relevant site is worth more than dozens from low-quality directories
- Never buy links or participate in link schemes
- Diversify anchor text naturally
- Focus on earning links to specific pages (service pages, blog posts) not just the homepage
- Track backlink growth in Google Search Console or Ahrefs/Semrush
