### Guerrilla Office-Entrance Demo to Warm Corporate Event Rental Outreach [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GdcN5lRv6kI) · Dec 2024
`guerrilla-demo`, `b2b-cold-outreach`, `event-rental`, `physical-proof`, `corporate-sales`
**What it does:** Deploys a portable inflatable photo booth or experiential rental product outside a target company's office entrance, lets employees interact with it, then uses that warm exposure to dramatically improve cold email response rates.
**How to execute:**
1. Build or rent an inflatable photo booth or activation unit ($500-2,000 for entry-level; self-owned pays back in 3-5 bookings at $800-1,500/event).
2. Identify 10-20 target corporations within your metro area with 200+ employees ,  events and HR teams are the buyer; find their email via LinkedIn or Apollo.
3. Set up the activation outside the target's lobby during peak entry hours (8-9am or lunch); include branded signage with a QR code to your booking page.
4. Send cold email to the events/HR contact the same afternoon: 'You may have seen our photo booth outside your office this morning ,  here is what corporate teams typically book us for and what one day looks like.' Attach photos from that morning's setup.
5. Follow up with a short video walkthrough and a calendar link for a 15-minute call.
**Why it works:** Corporate event buyers are risk-averse; physical proof of the product working collapses scepticism that cold email alone cannot. The morning setup creates genuine social proof ('I saw that thing in the lobby') before the email arrives. Source: Koerner Office. Status: Live.

### Value-First Influencer Outreach: Research, Deposit, Then Ask [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/_1W5KXNLzEQ) · Jan 2022
`influencer-outreach`, `cold-to-warm`, `relationship-building`
**What it does:** Converts cold influencer outreach into warm introductions by leading with a genuine value deposit or mutual connection before making any ask, dramatically improving response rates.
**How to execute:**
1. Before reaching out, research what the influencer cares about beyond their main content: side projects, causes, recent podcast appearances, stated goals.
2. Find a mutual contact who can introduce you, or identify a genuine value exchange (a resource, a connection, a mention) you can offer first.
3. If cold DM is unavoidable, open with one sentence referencing something specific about their work ,  not a compliment, a specific observation.
4. Keep the initial message to one short sentence; do not pitch in the first message.
5. After they respond, make the relationship real before asking: share something useful, engage with their content publicly, or make the introduction happen.
6. Only after two to three genuine deposits, make a single specific ask.
**Why it works:** Most outreach fails because it opens with the sender's need; a value-first frame shifts the influencer from 'who is this person asking me for something' to 'this person understands my work.' Warm introductions compress this sequence from weeks to one contact. Source: Leveling Up. Status: Live ,  value-first outreach principles are platform-agnostic and remain the most effective cold-to-warm conversion pattern.

### Nine-Word Re-Engagement Email for Cold Leads [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/AiKYGtWyqYQ) · May 2025
`email`, `re-engagement`, `cold-leads`, `sales-followup`
**What it does:** Resurrects leads who previously said no by sending a single nine-word check-in email ,  "Are you still in the market for [X]?" ,  months after the initial rejection.
**How to execute:**
1. Tag every lost deal or non-buyer in your CRM with a "timing-reject" flag at point of decline (vs budget, vs fit, vs genuine disinterest).
2. Set a 60-to-90-day re-engagement sequence: one email only, nine words: "Are you still in the market for [product/service category]?"
3. Do not include a pitch, a link, or a call-to-action beyond the question. The reply IS the conversion trigger.
4. When they reply yes, treat it as a warm inbound lead ,  they re-opened the conversation, so you follow with your normal discovery process, not a re-pitch.
5. Run this sequence via email, LinkedIn DM, or text depending on where the original conversation happened.
**Why it works:** Most buyers decline because of timing, not opposition ,  circumstances change in 60-90 days (budget cycles, new priorities, pain reaching a threshold); the minimal format signals low pressure and gets a response precisely because it asks nothing. Source: Leveling Up (Dean Jackson tactic, discussed with Neil). Status: Live ,  works across channels; low spam-signal due to brevity.

### Neutral Subject Line Tactic to Maximise Cold Email Open Rates [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/3jYMGZNuAmw) · Feb 2022
`cold-email`, `subject-line`, `open-rate`, `outreach`, `deliverability`
**What it does:** Replaces promotional or curiosity-hook subject lines with neutral, interpersonal-sounding lines ("Hi from Alex", "Quick question") to avoid the inbox delete reflex and earn the open.
**How to execute:**
1. Audit your current subject lines ,  remove any word that signals sales intent ("free", "offer", "growth", "results", product name, company name).
2. Replace with a short neutral phrase that reads like a personal email: first name only, a single-word topic, or a non-leading question.

**Why it works:** The inbox decision to open or delete takes under a second. A pitched subject line gets categorized as "vendor" immediately and deleted before the value inside is seen. **Why it works:** Email deliverability is a commons problem ,  bad actors on cheap shared IPs impose costs on all co-tenants. Premium providers solve this by curating their sender base, not by technical superiority. The price gap reflects the cost of maintaining a clean pool. Source: Leveling Up. Status: Live.

### Pre-Call ICP Checklist: Fix Cold Calling Results Before You Dial [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/yiRSBvPgQVA) · Nov 2023
`cold-calling`, `ICP`, `B2B-sales`, `prospecting`, `value-prop`
**What it does:** Shifts cold calling preparation from call technique to pre-call clarity — defining who, what pain, and why your product solves it before picking up the phone, so effort is spent in the right place.
**How to execute:**
1. Before any calling session, complete a three-question brief per prospect segment: (a) Is this person genuinely in my ICP? (b) What specific pain am I assuming they have? (c) Can I state the value prop in one sentence that connects to that pain?
2. If you cannot answer all three, do not call that segment. Rework the targeting or messaging first.
3. Research one recent, specific signal per prospect that confirms the pain hypothesis (hiring patterns, tech stack, news, job postings).
4. Use that signal as the opening line — not a generic opener about 'being in the area' or 'reaching out because we help companies like yours.'
5. After each session, score calls by whether the prospect confirmed the pain hypothesis — not just whether they agreed to a meeting. Refine ICP from no-pain responses.
**Why it works:** Volume without targeting is wasted effort. The pre-call checklist forces alignment on ICP, pain, and value prop before any calls are made, so the effort is spent digging in the right place. Source: Sam Dunning. Status: Live.
