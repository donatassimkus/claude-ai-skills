### 7. Pattern Interrupt in Ads [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/C1IPsE1C7ld/) · Dec 2023

`hooks`, `paid-ads`, `brand`

**What it does:** Break the expected flow of an ad or content piece with an unexpected element to stop viewers from scrolling or zoning out.

**How to execute:**
1. Identify the default expectation your audience has when they encounter your content format (e.g. standard ad opening, typical hook)
2. Design an opening that deliberately violates that expectation: unexpected visual, unusual statement, abrupt tonal shift, or out-of-context scenario
3. Walk the line between clever and distasteful: aim for surprising, not offensive
4. Tie the pattern interrupt back to your product or message so it is memorable, not just random
5. Test multiple variations: the riskier the interrupt, the higher the potential payoff but also the higher the chance of backlash

**Why it works:** Surprise triggers heightened attention and stronger memory encoding, making the message stick for years instead of being instantly forgotten.

### 100. AB test ad creatives [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/CrK5dy8NHlZ/) · Apr 2023

`paid-ads`, `conversion`

**What it does:** Run two ad variants with identical copy but different images to isolate which visual drives better performance.

**How to execute:**
1. Create two ad variants within the same campaign
2. Keep all variables identical (copy, targeting, budget, CTA) except one element (e.g. photo)
3. Test a personal/face photo against an attention-grabbing or humorous photo
4. Let both run with equal budget split until statistically significant
5. Kill the loser and scale the winner

**Why it works:** Isolating a single variable per test gives you clean data on what actually moves the metric, and face vs. non-face is one of the highest-impact image variables to test first.
