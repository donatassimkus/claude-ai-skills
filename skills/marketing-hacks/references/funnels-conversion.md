### 3. Bundle existing offers branded [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/C-V93XviR3c/) · Aug 2024

`monetization`, `conversion`, `brand`

**What it does:** Repackage existing products or services under a single branded bundle name to increase perceived value and simplify the buying decision.

**How to execute:**
1. Audit your current product or service lineup for items that can be grouped
2. Create a single branded name for the bundle (e.g. 'Starter Pack', 'Growth Kit')
3. Price the bundle at a round, simple number that feels like a deal
4. Promote the bundle as one thing rather than listing individual components
5. Test bundle vs individual pricing to measure conversion lift

**Why it works:** McDonald's dollar menu items all existed before the bundle; combining them under one branded name made them simpler to choose and drove significantly more sales.

### 14. Benefit-Framed Referral Program [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/C4MUdD4C-Qk/) · Mar 2024

`funnels`, `conversion`, `audience-building`

**What it does:** Name and frame your referral program around what the user gets, not what you want them to do.

**How to execute:**
1. Identify the core reward your referral program offers (storage, credits, features)
2. Name the CTA after the user benefit, not the action (e.g. 'Get more storage' instead of 'Invite your friends')
3. Place this benefit-framed CTA prominently in the product UI
4. Make the reward immediate and tangible so users feel the value before referring

**Why it works:** Users act on self-interest: framing referrals as a personal benefit rather than a favor increases participation because it answers 'what's in it for me' instantly.

### 21. Archetype Video Nurture Funnel [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/C61g4AaiuYw/) · May 2024

`funnels`, `youtube-growth`, `conversion`

**What it does:** Create YouTube videos targeting each customer archetype and send prospects the matching video between form submission and sales call to warm them up.

**How to execute:**
1. Define 5-10 customer archetypes based on demographics and goals (e.g. 'mother in her early 40s', 'single professional in his 50s')
2. Record a YouTube video for each archetype addressing their specific pain points and showing relevant client results
3. Build an intake form that captures enough info to segment prospects into archetypes
4. Set up automation: when form is submitted, match answers to an archetype and send the corresponding YouTube video link
5. Schedule the sales call 2 days after form submission to give the prospect time to watch
6. If prospect doesn't close on the call, send 2-3 more archetype-matched videos as follow-up

**Why it works:** Prospects feel personally understood before the call, which built enough trust to achieve a 50% close rate and allow shorter 15-minute sales calls.

### 48. Iterate Product Hunt Launches [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/Clt-Tk0LVIq/) · Dec 2022

`product-launch`, `conversion`, `audience-building`

**What it does:** Re-launch on Product Hunt after tweaking your landing page, features, and positioning rather than treating a weak first launch as final.

**How to execute:**
1. Run your first Product Hunt launch and track exact metrics (visitors, signups, conversion rate)
2. Analyze what underperformed: landing page copy, feature set, screenshots, social proof
3. Rebuild the landing page with clearer value prop and new features
4. Re-launch on Product Hunt as a new version or major update
5. Compare metrics directly against the first launch to validate improvements

**Why it works:** Their first launch got 9 users; after tweaking the landing page and adding features, the second launch got 40+ users from 570 visitors, proving that iteration on positioning and product beats a one-shot approach.

### 82. Freemium usage-gated funnel [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/CpPuIhWNe1Z/) · Mar 2023

`funnels`, `conversion`, `monetization`

**What it does:** Let users access the core product free with no signup, then gate continued access behind a free account, then gate expanded usage behind a cheap paid plan.

**How to execute:**
1. Make the core action (e.g. recording) available instantly with zero signup
2. Allow free users to share/use their output from that single session
3. Require a free account to access recordings beyond the current session
4. Set a low cap on free accounts (e.g. 10 recordings)
5. Offer a cheap premium tier ($5/mo) to remove the cap

**Why it works:** Each gate only triggers when the user has already received value, so the upgrade feels like preserving something they already have rather than paying for something unknown.

### 178. Disable dark mode for conversions [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DIRj7asiBzg/) · Apr 2025

`conversion`, `ui-design`, `ab-testing`

**What it does:** Default your app or website to light mode to maximize conversion rates, following the pattern of top transaction-focused apps.

**How to execute:**
1. Audit your app or website's current dark mode implementation
2. Check your conversion data segmented by light vs dark mode users
3. Set light mode as the default experience
4. If you keep dark mode, bury the toggle in settings rather than making it prominent
5. A/B test removing dark mode entirely and measure impact on transactions

**Why it works:** Top transaction-focused apps like Amazon, Airbnb, and Venmo have dropped dark mode, suggesting light mode drives higher conversion rates based on extensive A/B testing at scale.

### 185. Archetype-Based Video Nurture Funnel [source](https://www.instagram.com/p/DNuTKqd2AVi/) · Aug 2025

`funnels`, `video-production`, `conversion`

**What it does:** Segment prospects into archetypes via intake form questions, then send personalized YouTube videos addressing their specific problem before the sales call.

**How to execute:**
1. Run paid ads targeting your local audience with scarcity (limited spots, sold-out history)
2. Send ad traffic to a landing page with a short (under 60 seconds) intro video from the founder explaining the process
3. Include a form with questions designed to bucket prospects into archetypes (age, gender, goal, biggest problem)
4. At the end of the form, have them book a sales call 2 days out
5. Pre-record YouTube videos for each archetype: match the trainer to the prospect demographic, address the specific problem that archetype faces
6. Day before the call, have a sales rep review form answers, identify the archetype, and send the matching video
7. Run the sales call the next day with a prospect who already feels understood

**Why it works:** Prospects arrive on the call already trusting you because they received free, personalized value that proved you understand their exact situation.
