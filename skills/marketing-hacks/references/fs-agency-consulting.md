# Field-sourced marketing hacks: agency-consulting


### Hyper-Vertical Agency Client Acquisition via YouTube SERP Tutorials [source](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/ZlZdoCJebGk) · Jun 2024
`agency-inbound`, `youtube-seo`, `hyper-vertical`, `content-led-growth`, `serp-snack-pack`
**What it does:** Positions a Google Ads agency in one ultra-narrow niche, then ranks YouTube tutorials for '[service] for [niche]' queries in Google's SERP video block — attracting both DIY researchers who self-select into leads and hire-intent buyers in a single piece of content.
**How to execute:**
1. Pick one narrow niche (e.g. apartment complexes, HVAC companies, immigration lawyers) and commit to it as the agency's entire identity.
2. Research the 5-8 queries prospects actually type: 'Google Ads for [niche]', 'how to advertise [niche] on Google', '[niche] PPC tips'.
3. Record a clear, useful 8-15 minute tutorial for each query — no production overhead, screen recording is enough.
4. Optimise title, description, and thumbnail for the exact phrase, not branded terms.
5. Monitor Google SERP (not just YouTube) for your target queries — the video block placement drives hire-intent traffic that YouTube analytics won't show.
6. Close inbound leads by offering a free audit at the end of every tutorial.
**Why it works:** Hyper-specific professional services queries have near-zero video competition. Viewers who watch a full tutorial and still feel the work is complex are pre-qualified buyers — they have already tried to understand the thing they now want to outsource. Source: Greg Isenberg. Status: Live — YouTube SERP placement for niche professional service queries remains effective; competition has increased slightly since 2024 but verticals are still largely open.
