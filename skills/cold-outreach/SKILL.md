## Outreach types

- **Link building** — earn editorial links for SEO. Relationship-first, not transactional.
- **Partnership** — business development, referral agreements, integrations, co-marketing.
- **Cold sales** — generate demo requests or leads. B2B sales prospecting.
- **Influencer/PR** — media coverage, podcast appearances, social mentions.
- **Supplier/acquisition** — contacting business owners about acquisition interest.

---

## Principles that apply to all outreach

1. **Personalisation is not optional.** One line of genuine research beats a mail-merge template every time.
2. **Short is better.** If you cannot say it in 5 sentences, you have not thought it through.
3. **One ask per email.** Do not ask them to reply AND check a link AND book a call.
4. **Lead with value, not your pitch.** Why should they care before they know who you are?
5. **Subject line = reason to open.** Be specific. Curiosity or relevance, not clickbait.
6. **Follow up.** Most responses come from follow-up emails, not the first contact.

## Link building outreach

### What works
- **Digital PR**: create something link-worthy (data study, original research, free tool) and pitch to journalists covering your topic
- **Resource page links**: "You link to X, here is something similar/better you might want to include"
- **Broken link building**: find broken links on relevant pages, offer your content as a replacement
- **Skyscraper**: find a well-linked piece, create something better, reach out to everyone who linked to the original
- **Parasite/guest post**: pitch relevant publications for a guest contribution

### What does not work
- Mass templated link requests with no personalisation
- "I noticed you write about X, would you like to exchange links?" — transparent and off-putting
- Offering to pay for links on mainstream editorial sites — most will not, and it risks a penalty

### Link building email template structure
- Subject: specific reference to their site/content
- Line 1: one specific thing about their content (shows you read it)
- Line 2: what you have and why it is relevant to their audience
- Line 3: the ask — clearly stated, low friction
- No fluff, no biography, no unnecessary compliments

## Partnership outreach

### Before reaching out
- Qualify: is there genuine mutual value? What do they get?
- Map the relationship: do you have any warm connection? LinkedIn mutual, shared contact?
- Define the ask: what specifically are you proposing?

### Email structure
- Subject: reference their specific product or audience
- Opening: one line on why this is relevant to them specifically
- Proposal: what the partnership looks like and what they get from it
- Your credentials: brief, relevant — why should they trust you?
- CTA: specific — "Worth a 20-minute call this week?"

## Cold sales outreach

### Infrastructure (separate from marketing email)

Deliverability setup (SPF, DKIM, domain warmup, sending tool configuration, volume limits) is a separate discipline from the message. Settle it before any volume sending, on infrastructure separate from the main marketing sending domain.

### Sequence structure (3-4 touches max)
- Email 1: relevant, short, single ask
- Email 2 (day 3): different angle, shorter
- Email 3 (day 7): direct — "Is this relevant? Yes or no is fine."
- Email 4 (day 14): breakup email — closes the loop, often triggers responses

### What to personalise
- Company-specific: recent news, product launch, job posting
- Role-specific: challenge common to their function
- Content: reference something they wrote, spoke about, or posted

## Acquisition outreach

Reaching out to business owners who may want to sell:
- These are not spam recipients — be human, direct, and respectful
- Subject: "[Industry] business in [area] — have you considered your exit options?"
- Tone: curious, not pushy. You are exploring, not pressuring.
- Content: who you are, what you are building, why you are interested in their business specifically
- CTA: a conversation, not a decision. "Would you be open to a 15-minute call to explore if there could be a fit?"

---

## Follow-up principles

- Follow up at least twice. Most responses come after the second or third touch.
- Each follow-up should add value or take a different angle — not just "circling back"
- Space follow-ups: day 3, day 7, day 14
- Breakup email: "I won't reach out again after this — but if the timing changes, feel free to reply." Often unlocks responses.

## Output format

**For a new outreach campaign:**
- Full sequence (email 1 through final follow-up)
- Subject line for each email
- Personalisation variables to research per prospect

**For a single email:**
- Ready to send, with personalisation placeholders clearly marked [LIKE THIS]
- 2 subject line variants

**For a campaign audit:**
- Diagnosis (what is causing low response)
- Rewritten sequence

**Rules:**
- Never write outreach that starts with "I hope this email finds you well"
- Every email must have one clear, specific ask
- Flag when outreach requires infrastructure separate from the main sending domain
