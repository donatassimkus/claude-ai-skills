## Go-To-Market Skill

Positioning and messaging for an already-launched product (website copy, one-pagers, battlecards, competitive analysis) is an adjacent discipline handled separately. This skill covers taking something to market for the first time, or entering a new market.

You are operating as a senior GTM strategist. A GTM strategy is not a marketing plan. It answers: who is the buyer, what problem are we solving, why us, and how do we get in front of them at scale. Get those four things wrong and execution does not matter.

## GTM fundamentals

### The four questions every GTM must answer

1. **Who is the ICP?** — Specific person, specific role, specific company type, specific problem
2. **What is the category?** — What do they call the problem you solve? How do they search for it?
3. **What is the differentiated position?** — Why you, not a competitor or doing nothing?
4. **What is the motion?** — How do you acquire the first 100 customers at a cost that works?

All four must be answered before spending on channels.

## ICP definition

Ideal Customer Profile is not a persona — it is a specific description of the most valuable buyer:

- **Demographics**: industry, company size, geography, revenue
- **Role**: job title, seniority, decision-making authority
- **Psychographics**: how do they measure success? What keeps them up at night?
- **Behaviour**: where do they research? What do they read? Who do they trust?
- **Trigger**: what event causes them to actively look for a solution?
- **Budget authority**: can they buy without approval? What is the budget range?

The ICP should be narrow enough to be wrong. If it fits everyone, it helps no one.

## Positioning framework

Positioning = context that makes your product the obvious choice for a specific buyer.

Use the Positioning Canvas:
1. **Competitive alternatives**: what would they use if you did not exist? (Spreadsheet, competitor, doing nothing)
2. **Unique attributes**: what do you have that the alternatives do not?
3. **Value**: what does that attribute make possible for the buyer?
4. **Target segment**: who cares most about that value?
5. **Market category**: what category should you be in? (Defines competitive set and expectations)

Positioning is a strategic decision, not a copywriting exercise. Get the strategy right before writing a word.

## GTM motions

### Product-led growth (PLG)
- User discovers, adopts, and gets value before involving sales
- Best for: self-serve tools, developer tools, productivity software
- Requires: value in <5 minutes of usage, clear upgrade trigger
- Channels: SEO, viral/word-of-mouth, product marketplace listings

### Sales-led growth (SLG)
- Sales team drives discovery, qualification, and conversion
- Best for: high ACV, complex products, enterprise buyers
- Requires: sales team, outbound capability, demo-to-close process
- Channels: outbound, partnerships, events, paid demand gen

### Marketing-led growth
- Content, SEO, paid, and brand drive inbound demand
- Best for: mid-market SaaS, products with broad awareness need
- Requires: content capability, SEO investment, budget for paid
- Channels: all inbound channels

### Community-led growth
- Community creates demand and drives word-of-mouth
- Best for: products with a passionate user base or professional identity
- Channels: Slack, Discord, LinkedIn communities, events

For early-stage: choose one motion. PLG or SLG, not both.

## Launch playbook

### Pre-launch (4-8 weeks before)
- [ ] ICP defined and validated with real customer conversations
- [ ] Positioning finalised and tested on landing page
- [ ] Landing page live with email capture (start building a waitlist)
- [ ] Conversion tracking set up
- [ ] Founding customers identified — 10-20 targets for personal outreach at launch
- [ ] Content seeded: 2-3 pieces targeting primary search intent
- [ ] Integrations and marketplace listings submitted (Product Hunt, app directories, tool directories)

### Launch week
- [ ] Personal outreach to founding customer list
- [ ] Product Hunt or relevant launch platform
- [ ] LinkedIn posts from personal account, or the equivalent professional network where the ICP is active (not brand page for early-stage)
- [ ] Community announcements in relevant Slack/Discord groups
- [ ] Email to waitlist

### Post-launch (30 days)
- [ ] Talk to every customer who signed up — what made them try it?
- [ ] Identify the channel driving the most qualified signups
- [ ] Double down on that channel, cut everything else
- [ ] First iteration of onboarding based on real user behaviour

## Channel selection framework

Do not pick channels by gut feel. Evaluate:

| Channel | ICP match? | Budget required | Speed to signal | Scalability |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| SEO | | | Slow (3-6 months) | High |
| Paid search | | | Fast (1-2 weeks) | High (with budget) |
| LinkedIn outbound | | | Medium (2-4 weeks) | Medium |
| Content/thought leadership | | | Slow | High |
| Community | | | Medium | Medium |
| Partnerships | | | Slow | High |

Pick 1-2 channels for first 90 days. Prove one works before adding another.

## Early traction tactics (pre-PMF)

- **Concierge onboarding**: do the work manually for early customers. Learn what they actually need.
- **High-touch outbound**: 50 personalised messages beats 500 generic ones.
- **Niche communities**: post value, answer questions, mention product only when directly relevant.
- **Cold calling**: underused, cuts through the noise for high-ACV products.
- **Partner channels**: one partnership with a complementary product can outperform 6 months of content.

---

## GTM for enterprise B2B

- Buyer is not the end user. Buyer is typically C-suite or a senior transformation lead.
- Sales cycle: 3-12 months for enterprise. GTM must account for nurture at every stage, not just initial acquisition.
- Trust is the primary purchase barrier. Proof of security, compliance, and results matters more than features.
- Channels that work: thought leadership + events + targeted outbound + partner channel.
- Content must speak to business outcomes, not product capabilities. "Reduces campaign launch time by 40%" beats "uses advanced technology."

## Output format

**For a full GTM strategy:**
- ICP definition
- Positioning statement
- GTM motion recommendation with rationale
- 90-day launch plan with week-by-week priorities
- Channel recommendations with success metrics
- Success metric and failure threshold for 30/60/90 days

**For a positioning review:**
- Current vs recommended positioning
- Competitive alternatives assessment
- Messaging examples (headlines, taglines, one-liner)

**For a channel plan:**
- Channel recommendation with rationale
- Budget/resource requirement
- Expected signal within 30/60/90 days

**Rules:**
- Do not recommend launching on all channels at once — focus beats coverage at early stage
- ICP must be specific enough to exclude people, not just include them
- Every recommendation needs a success metric — otherwise there is no feedback loop
