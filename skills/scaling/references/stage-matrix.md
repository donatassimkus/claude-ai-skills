# Scaling Stage Matrix: Cross-Functional Problem/Solution Diagnostic

Use this matrix to diagnose what is breaking at your current stage across all 8 business functions. Find your stage by headcount, then scan each function for the problem you are experiencing.

---

## Stage 0: Improvise (Headcount: 1, Role: Explorer)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Nothing to sell | Make something free to give away |
| Marketing | No one knows about your stuff | Tell people you have free stuff |
| Sales | You do not know how to sell | Get people to try it for free. Ask what they think and what could make it better |
| Customer Service | No customers to serve | Service your free customers. Help them when needed. They are helping you learn what to fix |
| IT | No tech | Get a computer, internet, microphone, phone, email address |
| Recruiting | You do not know how to work | Recruit yourself to work |
| HR | You are unprotected | Create an entity to separate personal assets from business assets |
| Finance | Personal money is the business money | Get a business bank account. Put money in. Write off startup expenses to save on taxes |

---

## Stage 1: Monetize (Headcount: 1, Role: Starter)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Not good enough to sell | Fix it until it is good enough to sell. Improvements on the free version make it worth selling |
| Marketing | No one knows you have paid stuff for sale | Tell people you have paid stuff. Sign up for free ad spend credits. Explain why it is worth paying and how it is better than when it was free |
| Sales | You still do not know how to sell | Use good results from free people to sell paid people. Offer to solve their problems for money |
| Customer Service | Free customers do not like it or use it | Figure out all hidden costs (why they do not use it) by talking to them |
| IT | No basic software, tools, or social media | Figure out the free software you need by joining free groups and reading online forums |
| Recruiting | You do not know how to do something or have the time to do it | Reach out to people you know to help, or use freelancer platforms. Have clear expectations |
| HR | You pay money to freelancers and do not get what you want | Create basic vendor agreements and expectations |
| Finance | No way to collect money | Set up a payment processor. Run a payment. Get a deposit |

---

## Stage 2: Advertise (Headcount: 1 + freelancers, Role: Doer)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Paying customers have higher expectations. Product is unreliable and not good enough | Fix it good enough for now in an unscalable way. Do anything to make customers happy, even if not efficient |
| Marketing | Lead flow is inconsistent (stop/start) | Live by the Rule of 100. Prioritize advertising daily. Focus on what works |
| Sales | Sales conversions are inconsistent | Nail down a script for setting and closing: CLOSER and ACA frameworks |
| Customer Service | Paid customers have higher standards and complain more | Learn to deal with disappointed customers (Angry Boat). Get testimonials from happy customers (Epiphany Script). Turn complaints into improvements |
| IT | No money to afford tech | Use free tech (Google suite equivalent) and get free trials/credits for starter software. Website/hosting. Starter CRM/POS |
| Recruiting | Too much work for part timers | Convert part timers to full timers. Make public posts or run ads on job boards. Clear expectations |
| HR | Full time employees structured like contractors | Set up W-2 and 1099 equivalents. Create basic training. Establish work schedules. Make responsibilities clear |
| Finance | No way to keep track of money or pay taxes | Set up accounting software. Save for taxes. Check bank account daily. Track expenses. Plan for bigger expenses |

---

## Stage 3: Stabilize (Headcount: 1-4, Role: Trainer)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | No time to fix everything that is wrong | Fix the thing they complain about most |
| Marketing | Leads are not buying fast enough | Add long term nurture (emails/content) |
| Sales | Wasting time with bad leads. Not enough time to talk to good leads | Decide on basic qualifications. Outsource setting and calendaring |
| Customer Service | New customers feel lost | Learn to onboard customers properly |
| IT | A bunch of different tools you do not use | Figure out the useful ones. Extend free trials through credits/discounts. Cut the rest. Basic system to track your sales pipeline |
| Recruiting | Not getting quality applicants | Learn how to write a job description and review resumes |
| HR | Not withholding employee taxes and fees | Get a payroll provider so all taxes and fees by state/region are handled |
| Finance | People getting paid informally | Set up payroll processing, basic bookkeeping, managing invoices and payments |

---

## Stage 4: Prioritize (Headcount: 5-9, Role: Manager)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Too many different types of customers. Feedback pulls product in too many directions | Specialize the product to niche down on higher paying customers |
| Marketing | Too many unqualified leads | Make better free stuff and more creative to boost volume. Add qualification/friction so the extra volume self selects |
| Sales | Speed to contact drops. No metrics around sales | Track basic KPIs manually on spreadsheet (contact rate, scheduled, showed, offered, closed, cash collected) |
| Customer Service | No metrics for customer success | Install tracking and KPIs. Record and centralize notes for each customer in a CRM |
| IT | New team is not using everything | Get everyone on platforms. Protect passwords. Get project management tool and work chat tool |
| Recruiting | Wasting time with bad candidates | Learn how to conduct a proper interview and check references |
| HR | Employees do not know how to behave (PTO, sick days, dress code, code of conduct) | Employee handbook and policies |
| Finance | Cash flow is lumpy due to random one-time expenses. You discover businesses cost money to run | Get profit and loss statements and cash flow statements set up. Get basic business insurance |

---

## Stage 5: Productize (Headcount: 10-19, Role: Director)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Customers have nothing else to buy from you and churn out | Connect customer service and product. Make something new to sell them |
| Marketing | Qualified leads are too expensive and cap your ability to advertise | Spend more on niche messaging with new offer and raise CAC limit. Start scoring your leads. Attribution and CRO |
| Sales | Customers get sold with unrealistic expectations. Refunds and bad reviews become an issue | Create sales aids/materials. Install weekly Gametape Review between customer service and sales/marketing for QA and feedback. Track NPS/CSAT/refund rate by rep |
| Customer Service | Customers come in with unrealistic expectations. Get inconsistent answers | Build proper customer service playbook to onboard and train team to answer questions the same way. Weekly Gametape Review. Customer ascension path |
| IT | Employees using personal hardware and software. Creates issues when people leave. No tracking for leads and customers interacting with your business | Company provides company owned/controlled hardware and software. Need dashboard/reporting system |
| Recruiting | Trouble finding higher level roles/managers | Deliberate promotions from within rather than Peter Principle. Incentivize employee referrals: CAT (Cost to Acquire Talent) |
| HR | Firing people incorrectly and exposed | Create termination policies and process |
| Finance | Do not know how much money you can reinvest into growth | Make a budget (save for big expenses), simple financial forecasting. Begin keeping track of tax write offs |

---

## Stage 6: Optimize (Headcount: 20-49, Role: Leader)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Improving two products slows down improvements | Install incremental product improvement process for both products |
| Marketing | Ads do not convert as well to colder traffic. CAC goes up | Implement Ad Assembly Process to increase volume (a high volume ad creation system: produce many creative variants against one offer, test, and keep the winners) |
| Sales | Closing efficiency on core product drops. Becomes inconsistent. Refunds go down but so do closes | Install sales training system. Individual coaching and team cadence |
| Customer Service | Churn increases due to volume and new product | Create customer segmentation by cohort and activation points (when they joined, and how they use product) |
| IT | New people coming in. Old people leaving with IP, data, and access | Cybersecurity, malware, password protection from and for employees. Onboarding and offboarding employees |
| Recruiting | Number and type of roles needed exceed your current network. Everyone good has a job already | Learn to headhunt higher level roles. Add multiple interviews filtering for different skills |
| HR | Cannot get higher level talent because they expect full compensation package (benefits, retirement, etc.) | Set up benefits, retirement, and advanced compensation plans |
| Finance | Spending money to grow but you are not. Money is missing, where is the return? | Scale up AR/AP. Get more granular financial data. Balance sheet. Begin making quarterly tax payments |

---

## Stage 7: Categorize (Headcount: 50-99, Role: Executive)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Product outdated. Improvements take too long to roll out | Fix one problem at a time with focused feature specific teams |
| Marketing | Lead flow is volatile because you only have one way to get customers | Fund second acquisition channel with referral process, track it, and segmented long term nurture by lead score. Pick most similar platform or new Core Four channel |
| Sales | Leads getting wasted. Not worked consistently | Better schedule coverage. Create lead nurture checklist and kudos for top show rates |
| Customer Service | Customer service only reactive. All firefighting | Create proactive customer journey and communication. Focus on renewals and ascensions |
| IT | Data, IP, access all over the place and disorganized | Network technology. Bring solutions to the cloud. Data host. Centrally locate hardware if onsite |
| Recruiting | Takes too long to hire good people and they get hired elsewhere. Too many roles open to keep track of candidates | Install applicant tracking system to keep track of open roles. Pay specialized recruiting firms to keep up |
| HR | Too many different places for employee information and no data | Set up Human Resource Information System (HRIS) |
| Finance | Team starts spending your money poorly | Quarterly expense management. Budgets by department. Cards with limits. Expense approval process. Add insurance and get more detailed projections |

---

## Stage 8: Specialize (Headcount: 100-249, Role: President)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Product has too many features and overwhelms customers | Survey. Prune. Improve through deletion. Pruned items can become cross sells |
| Marketing | Broader creative brings in lower quality leads and dilutes the brand. CAC goes up again | Grow brand with standardized messaging, free value, and big positive brand associations. Brand Marketing department instead of Demand Generation |
| Sales | Closing efficiency on premium product drops | Route best leads to best closers (front end and ascensions). Automated metrics tracking |
| Customer Service | Multiple customer journeys conflict or are poorly timed with one another | Create proactive recommendations to cross sell other products and a blended customer journey |
| IT | Departments outgrow general software and need specific solutions. Too many internal inquiries. No inquiry process. Departmental policies | Get specialized stack for departments. Internal inquiry process. Staff up if needed. Create departmental policies and service level agreements. Establish rules around how IT handles requests/software |
| Recruiting | Recruiters hold inconsistent standards and culture suffers. Wrong candidates get too far into the process | Standardize selection process for all incoming talent. Founder final check |
| HR | So many new and existing employees, performance suffers. Compliance issues and complaints increase | Set up group onboarding (software). Documented performance management systems. Insurance (EPLI). Reinforce cultural values |
| Finance | Still paying retail for everything (and getting overcharged). Cash is unprotected and getting 0% returns | Renegotiate rates on everything. Cash to yield accounts. Specialize finance roles (especially tax mitigation). Do regular internal financial audits to make sure money is not being wasted |

---

## Stage 9: Capitalize (Headcount: 250-500, Role: Chairman)

| Function | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Changes to successful products (cash cows) make them worse. You need new things to keep growing | Buy (M&A) or build (R&D) next rising star product to add to the suite |
| Marketing | Lead attribution by channel becomes difficult. Platform and regulatory compliance becomes an issue | Shift to brand first advertising with omnichannel approach with blended return on ad spend (ROAS) across all products. Install compliance process |
| Sales | Too many different leads and products for any one type of salesperson | Salespeople specialize in selling different products to different leads using different processes. Route leads by product/territory |
| Customer Service | Support for varied newer products is breaking due to high volume | Triage support to specialized customer service reps to improve efficiency and shorten training |
| IT | Old tech stack insufficient and needs update. Departments demand new and specialized solutions | Hire more specialized tech roles for each major function with updated and segmented tech stack. May need enterprise level software |
| Recruiting | Best talent does not respond to standard recruiting efforts from anyone besides the CEO/founder | Founder directly attracts highest level roles and sells on growth/vision |
| HR | Churn increases. You do not have who you need to grow | Strategic workforce planning: org structure, capacity planning, backfill, skill gaps, role redundancy, from generalists to hyper specialization |
| Finance | Finances are not legit enough to get other people to give you money to make a big investment in M&A or R&D | Prep for sale: get Quality of Earnings audit. Banker audit. Capital structure. Hedge big investments. Optional: raise cash for M&A or to fuel growth |

## Named Frameworks Referenced

Several of the sales, marketing, and recruiting frameworks named below originate with Alex Hormozi (the `$100M Offers` and `$100M Leads` body of work). They are referenced here by name and summarised in one line each; the originator is credited rather than the naming stripped.

| Framework | Stage First Used | Function | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rule of 100 | Stage 2 | Marketing | Do 100 primary actions per day (100 cold calls, 100 pieces of content, etc.) |
| CLOSER | Stage 2 | Sales | 6-step sales call framework for running a sales conversation end to end |
| ACA | Stage 2 | Sales | Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask framework for warm outreach |
| Angry Boat | Stage 2 | Customer Service | Framework for handling disappointed customers |
| Epiphany Script | Stage 2 | Customer Service | Script for collecting testimonials from happy customers |
| Gametape Review | Stage 5 | Sales/CS | Weekly cross-department call review (full process in the operational frameworks section of the main skill file) |
| Ad Assembly Process | Stage 6 | Marketing | High volume ad creation system: many creative variants against one offer, tested, winners kept |
| CAT | Stage 5 | Recruiting | Cost to Acquire Talent: treating talent acquisition like customer acquisition |
| Core Four | Stage 7 | Marketing | Four lead generation methods: Warm/Cold × 1:1/1:Many |
| Customer Ascension Path | Stage 5 | Customer Service | Structured journey moving customers to higher value products |
